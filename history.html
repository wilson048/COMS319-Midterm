History Timeline

Building Bids -1964

Bids started to build the first complex for 4,500,000

Storm is Complete -1965

In 1965 Storms was complete, however due a strike it was only able to hold half capacity at the start of the year. And it ran into more issues as the year went on.

Knapp and Commons Built -1966

The Commons had actually opened in the winter of 1965. And quickly with both halls opened resident were not happy about the food. Knapp also has problems with the elevator and heating after opening.

Wallace is Built -1967

After experience Wallace hall was built with a budget of only 1,239,547. With bids happening in 1966. Much was learned from building the first complex but they still had issues with the elevators and heating.

Wilson is Built-1969

Both of the building were also built with a quite low budget of 3,820,000. Unlike the others though it seems that by Wilson most of the issues had been worked out and the building had few issues.

1969-2003

From 1969-2003 the buildings continued to function like most residence halls. There was nothing of note historical that happened during this time.

Storms Closes -2003

Due to structural issues and irreparable facade damage

Knapp Closes-2004

Following a simliar fate to its neighbor Knapp closed the next year.

Demolition of Knapp and Storms -2005

The video above shows it Click here. However, due to significant deferred maintenance and irrpearable facade damage. Due to the facade on the outside of the building falling off and being a hazard the buildings were taken down.

Wilson and Wallace Closed -2021

In 2021 due to low attendance partially due to COVID, though they expected it to stay low.

Wallace Reopens -2022

Due to an unexpected jump in enrollment Wallace was reopened to residence after only being closed for 1 year.

Wilson Reopens -2023

Due to an even more significant jump in enrollment Wilson was reopened after only being closed for 2 years.

Present

Now both Wallace and Wilson are back to full capacity. While their twins may be gone they are coming back as a vibriant and active place to live.

Historical Fun Facts

  • The land that was used to build the towers was originally a poultry farm.
  • The original plan was to build 3 complexes with two towers each. Sadly only 2 complexes ended up being built.
  • When Knapp was first built the roads around it were not paved. This lead to large pools of water which residence humourously put signs around saying no fishing or swimming.
  • Most of the Towers were built under budget. Which seems shocking compared to today where everything goes over budget.
  • The Knapp-Storms Complex was one of the first projects to financed with the sale of revenue bonds.
  • The towers drew much attention from other universities due to its efficient use of space which led to a low cost. It became an example for many dorms.
  • The first year that Knapp was open they had flooding in the elevator pits and the cold rooms quickly lead to the residents sleeping in hte commons
  • Both complexes were built with libaraies in the commons so that residents had access to books they needed without having to go all the way back to campus. It was even ran by the campus library.
  • In each of the complexes in one building there used to be housing for the head custodian and his wife when the buildigngs were first built.
  • When first built the towers were the tallest structure in Ames. Today Wilson is still the highest up due to being on a hill. However the tallest building in Ames is now an apartment on Stanton Ave, which beats the towers only by 2 feet.
